Reciprocal Intelligence: An offline-first, community-governed standard for intelligent systems in Indigenous territories.

Qori Labs is a non-commercial research initiative. We design systems that communities can own, govern, and shut down.

Concept only (no field deployment) Requires FPIC vivo

What is Reciprocal Intelligence?

It is a proposed design and governance standard to ensure AI and digital systems in rural territories serve community wellbeing, not extraction. It aligns with UNDRIP and Andean values to prioritize sovereignty over scale.

Read the RI Framework

Core Properties

  • Reciprocity as Obligation: Systems must return tangible value (Ayni) to the territory.
  • Community Authority as Law: Local assemblies hold binding power over system operation.
  • Offline-First Infrastructure: Systems run on community-controlled hardware, not the cloud.

Current Research Focus

Framework R&D

Developing the Reciprocal Intelligence standard, including governance patterns like Community AI Councils, Boundary Cards, and the Ayni License.

Offline Architectures

Designing LoRa and SMS-based emergency communication systems that operate independently of internet connectivity.

Lab Prototypes

Testing concept verification models in lab environments. No active pilots or deployments in communities.
